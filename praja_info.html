PRAJA — Unified Citizen
Governance Intelligence Platform

A national-grade, AI-powered public service CRM that predicts citizen dissatisfaction before it erupts, auto-routes and resolves grievances, performs multilingual sentiment analysis across 10+ Indian languages, and gives elected leaders a real-time AI co-pilot to govern smarter — built as a direct intelligent upgrade to CPGRAMS.

✓ Top Recommended Idea
👥 3–5 Member Team
Why This Matters

The Problem We Are Solving

India has 35 crore+ active internet users and 95 crore+ mobile connections — yet citizen grievance redressal remains broken, manual, and opaque. Elected representatives have zero real-time intelligence about their constituency's mood. Political decisions are made on gut feel, not data.

📋

CPGRAMS Is Failing

India's primary grievance system has an average resolution time of 45–90 days. Over 60% of complaints are "closed" without actual resolution. Zero predictive intelligence — purely reactive.

📊

Zero Constituency Intelligence

MPs and MLAs have no real-time data on what their constituents are experiencing. Decisions are based on surveys done months ago. By the time a problem becomes news, it is already a crisis.

🌐

Multilingual Barrier

India has 22 official languages and hundreds of dialects. Existing systems are primarily English and Hindi only, excluding the majority of rural and semi-urban citizens from effective participation.

⚡

Fragmented Communication

Citizens are forced to use 7+ different portals (CPGRAMS, PG Portal, CM Helpline, UMANG, state-specific portals) with zero integration or unified status tracking.

😤

Sentiment Blind Spots

Public sentiment shifts fast on social media but political teams lack real-time tools to monitor it at ward and booth level in regional languages. Issues go viral before authorities even know they exist.

🏛️

Market Size

India's e-governance services market is projected at ₹3.2 lakh crore by 2027. Every state government, 4,000+ municipal corporations, and 543 parliamentary constituencies are potential clients.

What We Are Building

3 Tightly Integrated Modules

PRAJA is not a single feature — it is three production-grade modules that work together to create a complete governance intelligence platform.

01

GrievanceOS — Omnichannel Citizen Complaint Engine

WhatsApp · Web Portal · Voice Call · SMS · Mobile App
Core Features
  • 📱
    Omnichannel IntakeCitizens submit grievances via WhatsApp, web form, voice (IVR), SMS, or app in any Indian language. Each submission gets a unique tracking ID instantly.
  • 🤖
    AI Auto-ClassificationNLP model auto-tags: Department (Roads, Water, Sanitation, Electricity, Health), Priority (P0–P3), Location (ward/block/city), and Sentiment (Angry/Urgent/Routine).
  • 📡
    Smart Routing EngineAuto-assigns to correct officer with SLA timer based on department + priority. High-priority (P0/P1) complaints are SMS-notified to senior officers immediately.
  • ⏰
    Automated EscalationIf unresolved within 72 hours → escalates to senior officer. At 120 hours → MLA/MP dashboard alert. At 168 hours → auto-flagged as "chronic issue" with pattern analysis.
  • 📸
    Photo Evidence SupportCitizens can attach images/videos. System uses vision AI to validate relevance (e.g., pothole, garbage pile, flooded street) and auto-generates structured descriptions.
What Makes It Different
  • 🔗
    Before & After Micro-AccountabilityWhen a ward issue (e.g., broken streetlight on Gali 4) is resolved, the system triggers a "Before & After" notification only to residents in that specific micro-zone.
  • 📊
    Department Performance DashboardReal-time SLA compliance score per department. Identifies systemic bottlenecks: "Water dept has 340 overdue tickets this month."
  • 🎯
    Beneficiary Scheme LinkageMaps citizens submitting complaints to government scheme beneficiaries (Ayushman Bharat, PM Awas Yojana). Allows targeted scheme outreach to eligible non-beneficiaries.
  • 🌍
    10+ Language SupportHindi, English, Tamil, Telugu, Bengali, Marathi, Gujarati, Kannada, Punjabi, Odia — using Whisper ASR for voice and IndicBERT/mBERT for text classification.
Demo Target: Show a citizen filing a complaint in Tamil via WhatsApp → system classifies it in 2 seconds → officer receives routed ticket → resolution with notification.
02

SentinelPulse — Real-Time Constituency Sentiment Intelligence

Social Media · News · WhatsApp Groups · Survey Data · Ground Reports
Core Features
  • 📡
    Multi-Source IngestionContinuous scraping from Twitter/X, Facebook Groups, regional news sites, public WhatsApp channels, and Reddit India — across 10+ Indian languages simultaneously.
  • 🧠
    Multilingual Sentiment EngineIndicBERT-based sentiment classifier detecting Positive / Negative / Neutral / Angry / Hopeful per topic per ward. Trained on Indian political and governance data.
  • 🗺️
    Live Public Mood HeatmapColor-coded interactive map at ward, block, constituency, and district level. Red = high grievance zone. Green = satisfaction. Orange = emerging issue. Drillable in real time.
  • 📈
    Trend Prediction EngineDetects emerging issue trends 3–5 days before they go viral. Sends proactive "Issue Emerging" alerts to administrators before public pressure builds.
  • 🔥
    Viral Alert SystemMonitors tweet/post velocity. When a local issue gains abnormal momentum, sends immediate red-alert to the elected representative with context summary.
Dashboard Capabilities
  • 📊
    Topic ClusteringAuto-groups complaints and posts by topic: Water, Road, Power, Corruption, Healthcare, Safety. Shows volume + sentiment per topic over time.
  • 🏘️
    Booth-Level GranularitySegments sentiment at the electoral booth level (India has 10.5 lakh booths). Identifies which exact booth has rising resentment vs. satisfaction.
  • 👥
    Demographic SegmentationCross-analyzes sentiment by inferred demographics: youth tech frustrations vs. farmer subsidy concerns vs. women safety sentiment — without violating privacy.
  • 📉
    Comparative AnalyticsTracks sentiment change over time: "This constituency's satisfaction score dropped 12 points in 30 days — primary cause: water shortage complaints (+340% spike)."
Demo Target: Show live heatmap of a constituency with drilldown → click a red ward → see top 3 trending issues → view individual social posts that triggered the alert.
03

NayakAI — AI Co-Pilot for Public Leaders & Administrators

Secure · Multilingual · Context-Aware · Real-Time Decision Support
Core Features
  • 📄
    Document IntelligenceUpload any government document (scheme PDF, budget report, policy circular) and get a 5-bullet summary in 10 seconds. Ask follow-up questions in natural language.
  • ✍️
    Speech & Letter DraftingAI drafts official responses, public speeches, inauguration scripts, RTI replies — in Hindi, English, or regional language — based on the context provided by the leader.
  • 📅
    Smart Schedule ManagerIntegrates with constituency calendar. Shows upcoming events with AI-generated preparation briefs: "Tomorrow: Inauguration at Sector 12. Here are 5 key achievements to highlight."
  • 🎯
    Constituency Intelligence FeedMorning briefing automatically generated every day: top 5 issues burning in constituency, which wards need personal attention, pending SLA violations, viral posts.
  • 🔔
    Real-Time Action Alerts"3 complaints about water shortage in Ward 14 escalated to P0. Here is a drafted response + escalation order to the Water Department ready to send."
Advanced Capabilities
  • 🗣️
    Meeting SummarizerRecord or upload public meeting audio. AI transcribes, identifies action items, assigns owners, and sets follow-up reminders within 60 seconds.
  • 📊
    Development Report CardAutomatically tracks and visualizes all infrastructure projects, scheme disbursements, and events in the constituency — generates shareable "What I Did This Month" reports.
  • 🔐
    Secure Access ArchitectureRole-based access control: Leader sees constituency overview. Officer sees only their department. Staff sees only assigned tickets. All data encrypted at rest and in transit.
  • 📱
    Mobile-First for Field WorkersLightweight mobile app for on-ground workers to log activities, update ticket statuses, and report from the field without needing desktop access.
Demo Target: Show "Morning Brief" dashboard → upload a 20-page scheme PDF → get 5-bullet summary → generate a speech for inauguration event using one click.

Immediate Priority

First Round — Unstop Application

The Unstop registration/application is your entry to the event. Your application will be evaluated and shortlisted before you get the exhibition booth. This must be compelling enough to get selected. Do this in the first 3 days (by March 6).

🎯 What the Unstop Application Must Contain

Think of this as your first pitch. Evaluators will see dozens of applications. Make yours stand out with clarity, specificity, and evidence of real thinking.

  • One-Line PitchWrite one powerful sentence that captures the entire product: "PRAJA is an AI-powered, multilingual citizen grievance and constituency intelligence platform that replaces CPGRAMS with real-time sentiment heatmaps, automated routing, and an AI co-pilot for elected leaders."
  • Problem Statement SelectionSelect "Smart Public Service CRM (PS-CRM)" as your primary problem from Domain 2: Digital Democracy. You can mention that you are also addressing AI Co-Pilot + Sentiment Analysis in the same platform.
  • Solution Summary (200–300 words)Describe all 3 modules clearly. Mention the specific pain points being solved — CPGRAMS failure rate, 45-day resolution time, zero constituency intelligence for leaders. Include specific Indian statistics.
  • Tech Stack MentionList key technologies: FastAPI, React, IndicBERT, Whisper, Twilio, PostgreSQL, D3.js. Shows technical seriousness to evaluators.
  • Team CompositionList each team member's role clearly. Evaluators want to see that you have the skills to actually build this. Include: Full-Stack Dev, AI/ML Engineer, UI/UX Designer, Domain Researcher.
  • Impact StatementQuantify the impact. Example: "If deployed across India's top 50 cities, PRAJA can reduce average grievance resolution time from 45 days to 5 days, covering 43 crore+ citizens."
  • Scalability MentionState how the solution scales: "Microservices architecture allows deployment from a single ward office to a national-level installation without code changes."
  • Early Prototype / Wireframe (Bonus)If you have even a Figma wireframe or a basic working screen by Day 3, attach a screenshot. This significantly improves shortlisting chances.
Critical Rule: Teams who get selected are those who show they have ALREADY started thinking and building — not those who are planning to start. If you have a 1-page architecture diagram ready, attach it. Visual evidence of thinking = higher shortlisting probability.

Risk Management

Risks & Mitigation Strategies

Identifying risks early is what separates prepared teams from those who scramble on demo day.

Risk
AI Model Too Slow for Live Demo
High Severity
Probability
High — IndicBERT inference on CPU can take 3–8 seconds per request on average laptops.
Impact
Demo looks slow and unimpressive to judges who expect instant response.
Mitigation
Pre-cache top 20 demo inputs and their AI outputs. Show spinner UI with "AI Processing…" loading state. Use GPU Colab to pre-generate and store in DB for instant retrieval.
Risk
Twitter API Unavailable / Rate Limited
High Severity
Probability
High — Twitter API v2 free tier has strict limits. Paid access required for streaming.
Impact

Anticipated Judge Questions + Ideal Answers
Q: How is this different from CPGRAMS or the CM Helpline?
CPGRAMS is a form-submission system with no AI, no multilingual support, no sentiment intelligence, and no predictive capability. It is reactive. PRAJA is proactive — it predicts issues before citizens even file complaints, supports 10+ languages natively, routes intelligently in seconds, and gives elected leaders a real-time intelligence layer that CPGRAMS does not have. We are not competing with CPGRAMS. We are its successor.
Q: How will you handle data privacy for citizens?
All citizen grievance data is stored on government-controlled infrastructure, encrypted at rest using AES-256 and in transit using TLS 1.3. Sentiment analysis uses public social media posts — no private data. Role-based access ensures a street-level officer can only see their assigned tickets, not the entire constituency data. We are compliant with India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 architecture by design.
Q: What happens when a politician uses this to profile voters politically?
PRAJA is a governance tool, not a political targeting tool. The sentiment and grievance data is town-aggregated at ward level — it shows which issues are hot in which areas, not individual citizen data. The system has audit logs for every data access, and administrator-level use is auditable by the Election Commission. The design is based on issue-tracking, not voter profiling.
Q: What is your go-to-market strategy if you win?
Three parallel paths: First, government procurement — approach state IT departments and NIC to pilot in 2–3 municipal corporations. Second, political party CRM — license it as a constituency management tool to parties directly. Third, MLA/MP direct adoption — offer a freemium model where the basic tier is free for elected representatives, with premium analytics as paid. The first paid pilot can be up in 6 months with a team of 8.
Q: Can it scale to all 96 crore voters / 10.5 lakh booths?
The architecture is designed for it. We use a microservices pattern — each module (GrievanceOS, SentinelPulse, NayakAI) can scale independently. The sentiment engine can process 100,000 posts per hour on a 3-node cluster, which covers all major Indian metropolitan areas in real time. Full national deployment with NIC infrastructure and government cloud is achievable within 18 months of a funded rollout.
