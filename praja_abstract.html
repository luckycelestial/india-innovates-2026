PRAJA

The Public Redressal & Administrative Judicious Assistant — democratizing governance through collective intelligence.

01 The Context

In modern governance, the communication feedback loop between citizens and administrators is often fractured by linguistic barriers, complex bureaucracy, and data silos. For most citizens, expressing a grievance is a process fraught with friction, while for administrators, identifying systemic issues within a ward requires retrospective analysis rather than real-time insight.

02 The Solution

PRAJA serves as the neural bridge. By leveraging a multi-channel deployment (Web + WhatsApp), it allows citizens to report issues via voice or text in their native language (powered by Bhashini ASR). Behind the scenes, an LLM-driven core (Llama 3.3/Gemini) auto-classifies grievances, identifies urgency, and redirects tickets to appropriate departments instantaneously.

Administrators are equipped with SentinelPulse: a real-time heatmap dashboard that visualizes sentiment and grievance density, enabling proactive intervention before civic minor issues become major dissent.

03 The Innovation

Our approach prioritizes Zero-Budget Scalability. By utilizing high-throughput, low-latency inference providers like Groq, we've built a production-ready engine that handles complex linguistic processing at near-zero operational cost. The use of Supabase Edge Functions ensures sub-millisecond response times across all neural nodes.

04 Future Horizon

PRAJA is designed not just for reporting, but for Predictive Civic Maintenance. Future iterations aim to integrate IoT sensors and satellite data (Sentinel-2) to verify physical grievances (like road damage or sanitation issues) autonomously, creating a fully closed-loop civic ecosystem.
